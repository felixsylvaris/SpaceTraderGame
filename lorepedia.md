# 🌌 Spice Space Trader — Lorepedia

*In-universe reference document. Not official Dominion Fleet material. Accuracy not guaranteed. Author may have been drinking Void Pepper Whiskey.*

## 🌍 Terra
**Class:** Terran Standard | **Role:** Capital, Administrative Hub, Agricultural Core

Terra is the seat of the Dominion Fleet and the closest thing the inhabited galaxy has to a political centre. It is also, depending on who you ask, either the cradle of civilisation or the most aggressively bureaucratic rock in known space.

**Geography & Climate**
Temperate and largely pleasant. Significant landmass dedicated to managed farmland, most famously the great cinnamon orchards that stretch across the equatorial belt. The orchards smell extraordinary and are considered a protected cultural asset, which makes every wildfire season a political crisis as much as an agricultural one. Cities are clean, well-administered, and slightly smug about it.

**Economy**
Cinnamon is the flagship export and point of civic pride. Terra produces more of it than any other planet and the farming families have been at it for generations. Beyond spice agriculture, Terra hosts the Dominion Fleet administrative complex, several major research universities, and a pharmaceutical sector that officially has nothing to do with Void Pepper and would appreciate you not asking.

**Dominion Fleet**
Fleet HQ is on Terra. Recruitment offices on every corner. The Fleet is the largest employer on the planet and the largest single political force in the galaxy. It maintains the jump lanes, arbitrates trade disputes (in theory), and fights pirates (also in theory). Relations between the Fleet and the outer colonies have been described as "complicated" by diplomats and "actively hostile" by everyone else.

**Culture**
Terra considers itself the cultural standard by which other planets are measured. Other planets find this attitude charming in the way that a large, self-satisfied dog is charming. The Cinnamon Roll Festival in Mari is genuinely beloved across the galaxy, even by people who resent everything else Terra stands for. The thirty-metre roll is real. The street cleanup takes all of Aprix. This has happened every year for two hundred years.

**Points of Note**
- Home of the Fleet Supply Depot — military logistics infrastructure embedded in civilian port
- The Side Hold ship upgrade is a Terra exclusive, manufactured in the orbital dockyards
- Long Range Radar units are produced by a Terra-based defence contractor with a very cosy Fleet contract
- Cinnamon farm ownership is considered the traditional retirement for successful independent traders

## ⚙️ Zeta-9
**Class:** Industrial | **Role:** Manufacturing Hub, Shipyard, Mining Operation

Zeta-9 is where things are built. Ships, components, refined metals, precision instruments — if it requires a factory floor and someone willing to work a twelve-hour shift, it comes from Zeta-9. The planet does not pretend to be beautiful. It has long since stopped trying.

**Geography & Climate**
Originally a resource-rich world with functional if unremarkable vegetation. Decades of aggressive strip-mining have left significant portions of the surface barren, scarred, and vaguely the colour of an old bruise. The remaining vegetation clings on in designated green zones that the mining corporations maintain for regulatory compliance purposes and photograph heavily for their annual reports. Air quality in industrial districts requires filtration gear. The locals have stopped noticing.

**Economy**
Zeta-9 runs on extraction and manufacture. Saffron is the prestige agricultural export — grown in controlled hydroponic belts away from the industrial zones, and considered exceptional quality precisely because the conditions required to produce it are so carefully maintained against the surrounding industrial chaos. The shipyards are the planet's true economic engine. Every major Dominion Fleet vessel has Zeta-9 components. Most independent trader ships were either built here or repaired here at some point.

**The Mining Problem**
This is the thing nobody in the boardrooms likes to discuss openly: the easy ore is gone. The shallow seams played out two generations ago. Current extraction requires going deeper, using more expensive equipment, dealing with increasingly unstable geology. Profit margins are tightening. The corporations are aware. Their shareholders are not yet aware. This situation will eventually resolve itself in one of several ways, none of them calm.

**Culture**
Zeta-9 has a working-class pragmatism that other planets interpret as unfriendliness and Zeta-9 natives interpret as not having time for nonsense. The Golden Ginger Gala is the one month a year the planet officially relaxes. Ginger root costumes. Hovercart racing through the market district. The winner gets a barrel of Saffron Mead. It is aggressively, almost defiantly fun, which tells you something about the other eleven months.

**Points of Note**
- The Mining Laser upgrade is manufactured and sold exclusively through Zeta-9 industrial suppliers
- The Galactic Broker License exam is conducted on Nexus, but most serious traders study using Zeta-9 market data
- Saffron from Zeta-9 commands a premium — grown against the odds, which apparently makes it taste better
- Recent geological surveys have been quietly classified by three separate corporations simultaneously

## 🌿 Agrica
**Class:** Jungle World | **Role:** Agricultural Frontier, Archaeological Zone, Contested Territory

Agrica is what happens when a planet has too much of everything. Too much jungle. Too much oxygen. Too much wildlife. Too much ancient history that nobody fully understands and everybody has an opinion about. It is chaotic, fecund, dangerous, and produces some of the finest paprika in the known galaxy, which somewhat justifies the giant scorpions.

**Geography & Climate**
The Chopchop Forest covers approximately seventy percent of the landmass and has been formally mapped approximately thirty percent of the time. What has been mapped keeps changing because the forest grows back faster than surveying teams can work. Climate is hot, humid, and enthusiastically alive. Oxygen content is notably higher than Terran standard, which makes settlers feel energetic and makes fires catastrophic. Several fires are happening at any given moment. The locals treat this as background noise.

**The Bugs**
Agrica's insect life has had millions of years to scale up in the high-oxygen environment. The results are significant. Cockroaches the size of a large dog are considered a minor nuisance. The giant scorpions — locally called Crushers — reach the size of a ground vehicle and are classified as a wildlife management concern rather than a predator threat, mostly because calling them predators would require admitting how many settlers they eat. Corporation Pandora's ongoing experiment to bioengineer local insects into Void Pepper production is continuing. The specimens keep escaping into the jungle and getting smarter. This is being monitored.

**The Lionids**
Agrica's native sapient species. Bipedal, feline in general morphology, with a social structure historically organised around pride groupings and territorial ranges. First contact with Dominion explorers went poorly in the way that first contacts generally go poorly when one side has orbital capability and the other does not. Relations have improved since, unevenly. Some Lionid prides have integrated into settler society — Whitemaine is a well-known example of the type, operating as an independent mercenary across multiple planets. Other prides remain in the deep jungle and have made their preferences regarding visitors clearly understood, usually through the visitors not returning.

**The Ruins**
Throughout the jungle, beneath the jungle, and occasionally falling out of the jungle when a particularly old tree comes down: ruins. Stone structures of extraordinary scale and precision, predating any known Lionid civilisation by a margin that makes archaeologists go quiet and then start arguing. The Lionid oral traditions reference the ruins as sacred sites built by ancestor-spirits. A competing school of thought, favoured by a subset of xenoarchaeologists and a much larger subset of people who find ordinary history insufficiently dramatic, attributes them to the Progenitors — a hypothetical ancient spacefaring civilisation whose existence has been proposed to explain several anomalies across multiple planets and confirmed precisely nowhere.

Expeditions into the deep jungle to investigate the ruins go missing at a rate that should discourage further expeditions. It does not.

**Economy**
Paprika. Vast quantities of it. Agrica's soil and climate are ideal, the growing season is essentially continuous, and the farming operations have learned to work around the Crushers with a combination of heavy fencing and aggressive resignation. The cinnamon berry substitute experiment is ongoing — local berries produce a compound with similar flavour profile to true cinnamon, which Agrica farmers consider a promising alternative revenue stream and Terra considers an insult.

**Culture**
Settler culture on Agrica is frontier pragmatic with a strong streak of competitive pride. If you survived your first year, you are respected. If you survived your first Crusher encounter, you are buying drinks. The Paprika Panic Parade is deeply traditional — the dummy, the plasma hammers, the spice shrapnel, the children scrambling for it. It has been described by outside observers as alarming. The locals describe it as Tuesday.

**Points of Note**
- The Grain Silo cargo upgrade is Agrica-exclusive, designed originally to transport bulk paprika efficiently
- Lionid integration is patchy — Nexus has the highest integrated Lionid population; Void Colony has almost none
- The ruins are officially an archaeological protected zone; this protection is officially enforced; enforcement is officially ongoing
- Giant scorpion venom has seventeen known medical applications and is not available through any legal supply chain Terra will acknowledge

## 🛸 Nexus
**Class:** Station | **Role:** Trade Hub, Financial Centre, Mercantile Free Port

Nexus is not a planet. This is important. Nexus is a space station — the largest permanently inhabited artificial structure in the known galaxy, built in a gravitationally stable point along the primary jump lane network, and operated under a mercantile charter that has been renewed, amended, argued over, and renewed again every thirty years for the last two centuries. It functions as a free port, a financial exchange, a black market, a diplomatic neutral ground, and, depending on the district, several things that don't have polite names.

**Structure & Environment**
The station is old enough that its original layout has been buried under two centuries of expansion, modification, and enthusiastic structural improvisation. Official maps are advisory. The inner rings are wealthy, climate-controlled, and smell of clove and expensive cologne. The outer rings are louder, cheaper, and more honest about what they are. Gravity is centripetal throughout, which new visitors find disorienting for about a day and then stop thinking about.

**The Spice Situation**
Everyone on Nexus uses Void Pepper. This is not technically legal under Dominion Fleet regulations. It is, in practice, completely universal. Traders use it to stay sharp during long negotiation sessions. Brokers use it to process market data faster. The station's cleaning staff use it because their shift patterns are inhuman and someone has to mop the outer ring corridors at three in the morning. The Fleet inspectors who periodically visit to enforce Void Pepper regulations are always offered a glass of something that is officially Clove Rum and contains, officially, only Clove Rum.

The consequence of this is that Nexus traders operate in a state of slightly elevated sensory perception at all hours, which makes them exceptionally good at their jobs and occasionally alarming to negotiate with. They will also pay almost anything for a guaranteed Void Pepper supply, a fact that independent traders have been exploiting for as long as the station has existed.

**Economy**
Nexus does not produce goods. Nexus trades goods, finances goods, insures goods, speculates on the future price of goods, and collects a small percentage of every transaction that passes through its systems, which collectively amounts to an enormous sum. The Clove trade is the closest thing to local production — Nexus grows clove in its agricultural sections and the Clove Rum distillery in the inner ring is a genuine point of pride. The Allspice Arbitrage Fête in Dedl is the annual peak of the financial calendar, characterised by formal wear, enormous bids, and the entirely predictable price crash the following month that everyone pretends to be surprised by.

**The Galactic Broker License**
The definitive trade credential. Issued by the Nexus Mercantile Authority, recognised across all Dominion-adjacent space, and studied for by traders everywhere. The exam covers market mechanics, legal trade frameworks, arbitration procedures, and a practical section on not getting obviously swindled. Costs 1,000 credits and is worth more than that within a few successful trades.

**Culture**
Nexus has the most cosmopolitan population in the galaxy — humans from every planet, integrated Lionids, a floating population of traders from the Outer Colonies, and several categories of residents whose origin is listed as "registered vessel" because they have not technically been on solid ground in years. The common culture is commercial: respect is denominated in credits, reputation is tracked in market performance, and the worst insult is a bad deal. The best compliment is that someone drives a hard bargain.

**Points of Note**
- Void Torpedo weapons are sold quietly through a Nexus broker despite being manufactured on Void Colony — the legal status is a matter of creative interpretation
- The Nexus black market is not located anywhere specific; it is located wherever two people with something to sell happen to be standing
- The Allspice price crash in Nova is so reliable that betting against it is considered proof of either extraordinary courage or Void Pepper overconsumption
- Several Dominion Fleet regulations technically do not apply within the station's charter jurisdiction; legal opinion on which ones varies

## ❄️ Void Colony
**Class:** Frontier Marginal | **Role:** Void Pepper Production, Deep Space Outpost, Uncharted Territory Gateway

Void Colony is the last significant settlement before the jump lanes thin out and the charts start using phrases like "estimated" and "believed to be." It is cold, understaffed, resource-rich in ways that have not yet been fully exploited, and home to the only known reliable Void Pepper production in the galaxy. These facts are related.

**Geography & Climate**
A marginal world — habitable, but not enthusiastically so. Cold climate, long winters, short growing seasons for anything that isn't Void Pepper, which thrives in conditions that discourage most other agriculture. The landscape is open, spare, and geologically interesting in ways that suggest significant subsurface resource deposits that nobody has yet had the workforce to properly survey. The sky has too many stars and they are too bright and the nights are very long.

**Void Pepper**
Everything about Void Colony's economy, politics, and cultural identity circles back to Void Pepper. The plant grows nowhere else in known space reliably. Attempts to cultivate it elsewhere have failed, succeeded once, failed again, and been quietly abandoned by parties who did not want the competition. Void Pepper requires specific soil chemistry, specific cold temperature cycles, and — according to the farmers who grow it — a specific attitude of determined suffering that the planet provides in abundance.

The Fleet officially classifies Void Pepper as a controlled substance. The Colony officially notes that it is their primary export and the Fleet is welcome to enforce that classification using personnel the Fleet has not actually stationed here. This is the core of the political relationship between Void Colony and the Dominion Fleet, compressed into one exchange.

**Governance**
The Colony has been through several governors. The previous one was arrested. The new one is more pragmatic about certain categories of commerce. The settlers broadly approve. The settlers are the kind of people who chose to live in a cold, distant outpost at the edge of the charts, which tells you something about their general attitude toward central authority.

**The Outer Edge**
Beyond Void Colony, the mapped jump lanes end. There are Outer Colonies — outposts, extraction operations, communities that exist in the records as coordinates and periodic supply requests. They communicate rarely. They trade occasionally. They are known to be out there. What exactly is out there beyond them is a question that gets filed under "future survey priorities" and has been in that file for forty years. Void Colony is where you go to ask questions about the Outer Colonies. The answers are usually "yes, they exist" and "no, we haven't heard from them recently."

**Culture**
Frontier minimal. Settlers here are self-selected for independence, high cold tolerance, and a preference for being left alone. The cantina — The Pepper's Shadow — serves Void Pepper Whiskey that has reportedly powered small starships, which may be a metaphor and may not be. The Void Vanilla Vigil in Octo is the annual communal occasion: a zero-gravity ceremony, a buried vanilla pod, collective weeping for the harvest. Outsiders find it strange. Settlers find it necessary. The bartender always cries.

**Points of Note**
- The Void Torpedo is manufactured here, extremely illegal elsewhere, and sold here without paperwork
- The Booster upgrade is a Void Colony exclusive — developed originally for emergency escape burns in pirate-heavy space
- Void Pepper consumption on Nexus is the primary driver of interstellar Void Pepper demand; the Colony is aware of this leverage
- The governor's office has not responded to three consecutive Fleet compliance audits; the Fleet has not yet decided what to do about this

## 🔭 The Outer Colonies
**Class:** Unknown/Varied | **Role:** Uncharted, Speculated, Occasionally Heard From

They exist. This is the most confident statement that can be made about the Outer Colonies as a collective. Beyond the mapped jump lanes, beyond Void Colony's outermost waypoints, there are settlements — founded by people who wanted to go further, or had reasons to leave, or simply followed a survey probe into the dark and decided to stay.

Supply runs reach them. Occasionally. Traders who have made the run describe long jumps, unreliable navigation data, communities that have been alone long enough to develop their own ways of doing things, and resources that have not been touched by corporate interests yet. This last point is of interest to several parties who are gathering information before gathering investment.

They do not appear on standard trade charts. They are out there. One day the lanes might reach them, or they might reach back. Until then, they are a rumour, a heading, and a question that the galaxy hasn't gotten around to answering yet.
